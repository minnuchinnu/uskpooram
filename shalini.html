WS03

Sketching backwards

-Murray Dewhurst

- Auckland, New Zealand

- Sketching backwards

Workshop Description

Working backwards on toned paper can free the sketcher from ‘white page fright’ and time consuming pencil work. After carefully choosing our composition, identifying important dark and light areas sketchers begin by pushing the shadows back into the page. Application is loose and free with little pressure because any ‘mistakes’ are easily corrected in the final stages. By pushing dark areas back we are creating a unifying underpainting – a base to push our highlighted subject forward. In the following stage our subject begins to emerge and the drawing tightens up. The sketch is completed by adding any necessary fine details.

Supply List

-toned, Kraft or colour loose pages or sketchbook

-opaque media such as gouache, Posca, Molotow pens (or similar) if available in bullet and wider chisel tips (White and pale blue are most useful)

-flat brush and water. A water brush is also useful

-black and white fineliners or your favourite fountain pen or brush
